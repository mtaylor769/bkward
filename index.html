Current Projects

The Grow

Logline: In the backwoods of the Northern California weed scene operates a malevolent presence, preying on unsuspecting victims used for material source to genetically power boost their product for mass appeal.

Genre: Horror/Thriller/Comedy/Stoner

Positioning Statement: The Grow takes the horror genre and twists it into a stoner, semi-comedic movie minus the usual goofball comedy associated with stoner movies. Your classic cabin in the woods but this time the unsuspecting victims are caught up in the nefarious world of a Satan worshipping, weed-growing cult. Join or die. This film fits into the emerging marketplace of cross-pollinated branded entertainment.

Tagline: Now that's some killer weed.

Target Audience: Primary Target: Males 18-50.

Secondary Target: Males 25-50. Crossover Audience: Females 18-50.

Rating: R (for language, sexual content and graphic violence)

Current Projects

Dirty Rats 1 & 2

Logline: A successful kidnapping plot perpetrated by a vengeful yet clever ex-con brings a savagely corrupt, drug addled/sex addicted Federal Judge to justice by forceful psychological reprogramming. The Judge’s disappearance sends his underworld cohorts into crisis mode as vice comes to a grinding halt. By the time the Judge re-appears, pandemonium reigns supreme.

Genre: Dark Comedy/Crime

Positioning Statement: Since the Mafia’s power has fizzled in recent times; this film is a different take on Mob comedies to include diverse factions of organized crime, corruption and calculated revenge.

Positioning Paragraph: True crime stories are America’s obsession. While this story is entirely fictitious, it is based on truth and most everyone loves a good mob story. As in real life, crime has become culturally diverse and colorful, as various criminal organizations are still actively pursuing and distorting their version of the American dream. When pursuit of the almighty dollar reaches new lows, Dirty Rats pushes the envelope straight into the gutter. Avoiding cliché’s and stereotypes, but still including the elements that make criminal life intriguing, this film promises to deliver. It makes no bones about including strong sex, drugs and violent content, yet takes a very light hearted stab at a world of corrupt scumbags that break the very laws they were hired to enforce. All politics aside, we can still laugh at the hypocrisy and idiocy we are exposed to at any given moment. The film can appeal to a broad audience and age group and include a modern soundtrack. It has primarily adult themes, with a healthy dose of screwball. Comparing to beloved classics, imagine Goodfellas meets American Gangster with Clockwork Orange and Boys in the Hood thrown in the mix, with the comedic overtone of The Hangover and even Caddyshack. A melting pot of treachery and douchebaggery much like the ever-crumbling society we seem to be living in now.

Tagline: With the rule of law in place, rules are made to be broken.

Target Audience: Primary Target: Males 18-104.

Secondary Target: Males 25-54. Crossover Audience: Females 18-55.

MPAA Rating: R (for language, sexual content and graphic violence)
